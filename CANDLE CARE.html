CANDLE CARE

When burning your soy candle for the first time, allow at least one hour for the wax to completely melt throughout the width of the jar. Because soy candles have a memory, a totally melted pool will provide an equal burn for future burns to encourage optimum smell release.

We strongly advise burning a candle for 2-3 hours in one sitting for the greatest results.
Please remember to trim the wick to 1/4 inch when using our wick candles. Before relighting your candle, use a wick trimmer tool or a piece of tissue to pull off the burnt black chunks of wood.
You can reuse the jar after your candle has burned to the bottom by removing the wick base and washing it with warm water and soap.

Frequently Asked Questions:

What causes the blackening of my candle glass jar?
Because of an untrimmed wick, your candle flame is burning too hot. Blow out your candle and let it solidify before trimming the wick. Before relighting your candle, use a wick trimmer tool or a tissue to remove the charred black parts of the wood wick. Remember to do this every time you relight your candle.

Why can't I relight my wood wick candle?
Before relighting, have you trimmed your wick? Blow out your candle and let it solidify before trimming the wick. Before relighting your candle, use a wick trimmer tool or a tissue to remove the charred black parts of the wood wick. Remember to do this every time you relight your candle.

My candle has burned all the way to the bottom of the jar. How can I repurpose it?
If you've used up your huge wick candle, you can scoop out the residual wax at the bottom of the jar and light it on an oil burner to keep the aroma going. Return the empty jars to AiA Candles's place to earn a RM1 rebate for each large jar or two small jars off your next purchase. You may also be creative and repurpose the jars to store makeup brushes, build a plant pot, or organise miscellaneous items around the house.

My candle has begun to tunnel. What should I do?
Wrap a layer of aluminium foil around your candle jar and light the wick for at least 2 hours, or until the fire develops stronger. This helps to concentrate the heat.
